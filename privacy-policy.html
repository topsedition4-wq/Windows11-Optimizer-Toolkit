Windows11 Optimizer Toolkit Privacy Policy

Version 0.1.0 | Publisher: Topse Development | Last updated: July 5, 2026

Overview

Windows11 Optimizer Toolkit is designed as a local Windows utility. The app does not require account registration and does not include telemetry collection, advertising tracking, analytics SDKs, or background data reporting to the publisher.

Information Processed Locally

The app may inspect local Windows configuration so it can show available optimization options and apply actions selected by the user. This may include Windows version, installed app packages, provisioned packages, services, scheduled tasks, registry settings, and related system state.

This information is processed locally on the device. The app may write local logs, reports, backups, and desired-state files to support transparency, undo, troubleshooting, and Update Guard behavior.

Information Sent to the Publisher

The app does not automatically send logs, reports, device configuration, personal files, or usage analytics to Topse Development. If you choose to contact support and provide logs or screenshots, that information is shared voluntarily by you for support purposes.

Network Access

The app is not designed to require publisher telemetry services. Windows, Microsoft Store, Microsoft Defender, Windows Update, GitHub, or other distribution platforms may process information under their own privacy policies when you use those services.

Camera, Microphone, Contacts, and Location

Windows11 Optimizer Toolkit does not request access to your camera, microphone, contacts, precise location, photos, videos, or documents as part of its normal operation.

Children's Privacy

The app is a Windows system utility intended for users who are old enough to manage device settings. It is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Data Retention

Logs, backups, reports, and configuration files are stored locally on your device until you delete them, uninstall the app, or use app-provided cleanup options where available.

Your Choices

You can uninstall the app using Windows app removal tools or the included uninstaller. You can also remove Update Guard separately using the app controls or included script. Local logs and backups may remain unless you choose to delete them.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when the app changes. The current version will be published with the app listing or project support page.
